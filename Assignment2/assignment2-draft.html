FATAL POLICE SHOOTINGS

From 2015 - 2022, 7743 people have been killed by police shootings in the United States. In this visualisation we will explore these fatal police shootings and their victims

1054 people were killed by police in 2021



21% of Victims showed signs of mental illness

Only 4.5% of the victims were females


Imbalance between different Race/Ethnicity Groups

Even though Black people account for 12.4% of the total population in US, they accounted for 27.27% of victims of police shootings.

While, white people account for 59.2% of the total population and they are 51% of the victims of death by police shootings

Native Americans are also victims of police shootings at a disproportionate rate when compared to thier percentage of population in the US

On average, in 83% of fatal police shootings, the body camera on the police officier was turned off.


A black person is more likely to be killed by a police officer than a white person

In most states, black people have the highest rate of deaths per 100,000 population
